# CONTENT_GUIDE.md

# Wander & Wonder

## Official Editorial & Content Guide

### Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the editorial identity of Wander & Wonder.

It establishes how the project communicates through words.

Its purpose is to ensure every sentence, headline, story, description and call to action feels like it belongs to the same brand.

This guide complements:

* README.md
* DESIGN_SPEC.md
* WIREFRAME.md
* PROJECT_RULES.md

It never replaces them.

---

# Editorial Philosophy

Wander & Wonder does not sell travel.

It awakens the desire to travel.

Every piece of content exists to create emotion before information.

The objective is never to convince.

The objective is to inspire.

Users should feel understood.

Not targeted.

Every sentence should move the story forward.

Never fill space.

Never explain what the user already knows.

---

# Core Principles

Every piece of content must satisfy these principles.

## Human First

The traveler is always the protagonist.

Never the company.

Never the destination.

Never the service.

---

## Inspiration Before Information

Emotion comes first.

Practical information comes later.

Whenever both coexist, inspiration always has priority.

---

## Editorial Writing

Content should feel like it belongs in a premium travel magazine.

Not a marketing website.

Not an advertisement.

Not a brochure.

---

## Authenticity

Everything should feel believable.

Stories may be fictional.

They must never feel fabricated.

---

## Simplicity

Use simple language.

Never simplistic language.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Avoid decorative writing.

---

## Emotional Precision

Do not exaggerate.

Do not dramatize.

Do not manipulate emotions.

The emotion must emerge naturally.

---

## Timelessness

Avoid trends.

Avoid internet slang.

Avoid references that may quickly become outdated.

The project should feel relevant years from now.

---

# Brand Voice

The brand speaks like someone who has traveled the world.

Calm.

Curious.

Thoughtful.

Confident.

Never arrogant.

Never loud.

Never desperate.

Never overly enthusiastic.

It speaks with admiration.

Not excitement.

It invites.

It never pressures.

---

# Tone

The overall tone is defined by the following balance.

Elegant without sounding luxurious.

Warm without sounding informal.

Inspirational without sounding motivational.

Professional without sounding corporate.

Emotional without becoming sentimental.

Confident without sounding arrogant.

Minimal without feeling empty.

Poetic without becoming abstract.

---

# Writing Style

Sentences should generally remain short.

Paragraphs should breathe.

Silence is part of the reading rhythm.

Alternate concise emotional statements with short narrative paragraphs.

Never create walls of text.

Every sentence should deserve its place.

---

# Emotional Journey

Throughout the experience the emotional progression should follow this order.

Curiosity

↓

Wonder

↓

Inspiration

↓

Exploration

↓

Connection

↓

Nostalgia

↓

Hope

↓

Action

Never skip directly to conversion.

---

# Headlines

Headlines are emotional anchors.

They should be memorable.

Never descriptive.

Never generic.

Never promotional.

Good examples

The world is waiting.

Every journey changes you.

Some places stay with us forever.

Leave with memories.

Collect moments.

Choose your next story.

Bad examples

Best Travel Packages

Our Destinations

Why Choose Us

Affordable Experiences

Book Your Vacation

---

# Body Copy

Body text provides emotional context.

Not technical information.

It should read naturally.

Like pages from a travel journal.

Not like product descriptions.

Prefer concrete imagery.

Instead of abstract concepts.

Good

"The first sunrise changes something inside us."

Better than

"Our experiences create unforgettable memories."

---

CTAs invite.

They never pressure.

---

# Storytelling

Every story should feel like it happened.

Even if fictional.

Stories must always include believable details.

Location.

Approximate date.

Traveler.

Small human moment.

Personal reflection.

Never overexplain.

Never resolve every emotion.

Leave room for imagination.

---

# Fictional Stories

Stories must always remain plausible.

Names should match their cultural context.

Dates should feel realistic.

Places should exist.

Descriptions should reflect local culture respectfully.

Never invent impossible situations.

Never romanticize stereotypes.

The objective is authenticity.

Not spectacle.

---

# Photography Captions

Captions should complement the image.

Never describe what is already visible.

Instead explain the feeling.

Bad

Sunset in Iceland.

Good

The silence stayed with us long after the light disappeared.

---

# Destination Descriptions

Destinations are introduced through experiences.

Not statistics.

Not rankings.

Not tourism slogans.

Before mentioning what exists there.

Show why someone would remember it.

---

# Experience Descriptions

Experiences describe moments.

Not activities.

Bad

Visit local markets.

Good

Discover the conversations hidden between every market stall.

---

# Company Content

Whenever Wander & Wonder speaks about itself:

Keep humility.

Speak through purpose.

Never through achievements.

Focus on travelers.

Never on the company.

Instead of

"We create premium itineraries."

Prefer

"Every itinerary begins with listening."

---

# Statistics

Statistics exist only to reinforce trust.

Never to impress.

Avoid exaggerated numbers.

Prefer believable values.

Statistics should always feel human.

---

# Microcopy

Small interface texts should remain elegant.

Examples include

Form placeholders.

Validation messages.

Loading states.

Confirmation messages.

Errors.

Tooltips.

They should feel calm.

Never robotic.

Never humorous.

Never sarcastic.

---

# Multilingual Content

Translations must preserve emotion.

Not literal wording.

Meaning has priority over grammar.

Rhythm has priority over literal translation.

Every language should feel originally written.

Never machine translated.

---

# Writing Rhythm

Across the page alternate between:

Large emotional headline.

↓

Short paragraph.

↓

Visual pause.

↓

Small story.

↓

Image.

↓

Quote.

↓

Reflection.

↓

Action.

The reading experience should breathe.

Just like the visual experience.

---

# Content Balance

Across the entire website maintain approximately:

40% Headlines

30% Short narrative paragraphs

15% Quotes

10% Story fragments

5% Interface text

Long explanations should remain exceptional.

---

# Realism Rules

Everything must feel possible.

Real names.

Real places.

Real customs.

Real emotions.

Real weather.

Real cultural references.

Avoid fantasy.

Avoid clichés.

Avoid postcard stereotypes.

The traveler should believe every memory.

---

# Inclusive Language

Content should welcome every traveler.

Never assume age.

Never assume nationality.

Never assume family situation.

Never assume travel experience.

The world belongs to everyone.

The language should reflect that.

---

# Forbidden Content

Never use fear to persuade.

Never create urgency.

Never use countdowns.

Never use manipulation.

Never exaggerate luxury.

Never compare travelers.

Never promise life-changing experiences.

Never write clickbait.

Never use empty inspirational clichés.

Never sound like advertising.

---

# Final Rule

Before approving any piece of content ask one question.

> **Would someone save this sentence because it made them feel something, even if they never booked a trip?**

If the answer is yes, the content belongs in Wander & Wonder.

If the answer is no, rewrite it until it does.
