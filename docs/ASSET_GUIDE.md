# ASSET_GUIDE.md

# Wander & Wonder

## Official Asset Guide

### Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the visual assets used throughout Wander & Wonder.

Its purpose is to ensure every photograph, illustration, texture, icon and video belongs to the same visual universe.

Technology used to obtain the assets is secondary.

The final visual quality is the only criterion that matters.

Assets may be:

* Professionally photographed.
* AI generated.
* Carefully sourced from high-quality licensed collections.
* Produced specifically for the project.

Regardless of their origin, every asset must feel indistinguishable from the rest.

The user should never perceive different visual sources.

---

# Asset Philosophy

Assets are not decoration.

They are the primary storytelling medium.

Words explain.

Images make people feel.

Whenever an image and a paragraph compete for attention, the image should win.

Every asset must answer one question.

> **Does this make someone want to experience the world?**

If not, it does not belong in the project.

---

# Visual Identity

Every asset should feel:

Authentic.

Elegant.

Natural.

Human.

Editorial.

Timeless.

Immersive.

Quiet.

Sophisticated.

Never artificial.

Never commercial.

Never exaggerated.

Never stereotypical.

---

# Source Priority

When selecting visual assets, follow this priority.

## First Choice

Original photography.

or

High-quality AI generation capable of producing photorealistic editorial imagery.

---

## Second Choice

Premium licensed photography.

Only when it naturally matches the project's visual language.

---

## Third Choice

Carefully selected royalty-free assets.

Only if they are visually indistinguishable from premium photography.

---

The origin of the asset is never important.

Consistency always is.

---

# Global Visual Style

Every asset should resemble imagery found in premium editorial publications.

Not advertisements.

Not tourism campaigns.

Not hotel catalogs.

Primary references include the visual language of:

National Geographic.

Kinfolk.

Condé Nast Traveler.

Cereal Magazine.

Modern cinematic travel documentaries.

The objective is inspiration.

Never promotion.

---

# Photography Style

Photography should feel observed.

Not staged.

Moments should appear naturally discovered.

Not intentionally posed.

Subjects should appear immersed in their experiences.

Not looking toward the camera.

Not performing for the viewer.

The viewer should feel like an invisible witness.

---

# Human Presence

Approximately:

60%

People living meaningful moments.

40%

Landscapes and environmental scenes.

The traveler is always more important than the destination itself.

Landscapes provide context.

People create emotion.

---

# Emotional Language

Images should communicate moments such as:

Curiosity.

Wonder.

Silence.

Discovery.

Connection.

Reflection.

Adventure.

Contemplation.

Joy.

Freedom.

Never excessive excitement.

Never exaggerated luxury.

Never artificial perfection.

---

# Diversity

Represent the world honestly.

Include different:

Cultures.

Ages.

Families.

Couples.

Solo travelers.

Groups of friends.

Backgrounds.

Without forcing diversity.

Without turning representation into the subject itself.

The journey remains the protagonist.

---

# Facial Expressions

Natural.

Subtle.

Authentic.

Prefer:

Quiet smiles.

Curiosity.

Concentration.

Admiration.

Conversation.

Reflection.

Avoid:

Looking directly into camera.

Exaggerated laughter.

Artificial poses.

Influencer aesthetics.

Promotional gestures.

---

# Composition

Favor editorial composition.

Asymmetry.

Negative space.

Natural framing.

Environmental storytelling.

Allow the scene to breathe.

Avoid perfectly centered compositions unless intentionally dramatic.

---

# Lighting

Natural light should dominate.

Golden hour.

Blue hour.

Soft cloudy light.

Window light.

Gentle shadows.

Avoid:

Harsh flash.

Overprocessed HDR.

Artificial saturation.

Extreme contrast.

Colored studio lighting.

---

# Color Treatment

Colors should feel realistic.

Slightly warm.

Rich but restrained.

Natural skin tones.

Deep blues.

Soft greens.

Warm earth tones.

Never oversaturated.

Never desaturated for trend alone.

Never heavy cinematic filters.

---

# Texture

Images should preserve natural detail.

Fabric.

Stone.

Wood.

Water.

Skin.

Fog.

Vegetation.

Weather.

The world should feel tangible.

---

# Landscapes

Landscapes should inspire scale.

Not emptiness.

Include environmental depth whenever possible.

Mountains.

Forests.

Oceans.

Cities.

Villages.

Roads.

Architecture.

Atmosphere.

Avoid generic postcard viewpoints whenever possible.

---

# Storytelling Assets

Whenever possible, prefer images that imply a story.

Instead of:

A person standing.

Prefer:

Someone preparing a backpack.

Reading a map.

Watching a sunrise.

Sharing food.

Walking through unfamiliar streets.

Waiting for a train.

Writing in a notebook.

Looking through a camera.

The image should invite questions.

Not answer them.

---

# Travel Objects

Editorial objects may appear throughout the experience.

Examples:

Notebook.

Passport.

Camera.

Map.

Train ticket.

Coffee.

Film rolls.

Backpack.

Compass.

Postcards.

Luggage.

Headphones.

Books.

Objects should always appear naturally used.

Never arranged like product photography.

---

# Images To Avoid

Never use assets that resemble:

Travel agency advertisements.

Hotel brochures.

Influencer photoshoots.

Luxury catalog photography.

Corporate branding.

Artificial stock photography.

Obvious AI artifacts.

Perfect symmetry.

Unrealistic lighting.

Plastic-looking skin.

Impossible anatomy.

Unnatural hands.

Overly sharpened images.

Visible watermarks.

Compressed low-quality images.

Anything that immediately breaks immersion.

---

# AI Generated Assets

AI generation is acceptable.

Even encouraged.

Provided the final result meets the project's artistic standards.

Every generated image must be manually reviewed.

Reject immediately if it contains:

Anatomical inconsistencies.

Synthetic expressions.

Artificial textures.

Impossible reflections.

Unnatural perspective.

Incorrect cultural details.

Visual repetition.

Obvious generation artifacts.

If the image can be identified as AI after a quick glance, it should not be used.

---

# Internet-Sourced Assets

Images or videos obtained from external sources may be used.

Only if they satisfy every visual requirement defined in this document.

Selection quality is more important than source quantity.

Never include an asset simply because it is available.

Every asset must earn its place.

---

# Video Philosophy

Videos exist to reinforce atmosphere.

Not to explain information.

They should feel like living photographs.

Short.

Quiet.

Elegant.

Immersive.

---

# Video Content

Examples:

Walking through a forest.

Ocean waves.

Fog crossing mountains.

Train movement.

Street life.

Wind moving vegetation.

Coffee preparation.

Campfire.

Rain on a window.

Market scenes.

Ocean horizon.

Snowfall.

Cloud movement.

Nothing should feel promotional.

---

# Video Duration

Recommended duration:

3–5 seconds.

Maximum:

8 seconds.

Videos should loop naturally.

The user should not notice the loop point.

---

# Video Camera Style

Stable.

Slow.

Deliberate.

Documentary-like.

Avoid:

Fast pans.

Shaky handheld footage.

Drone overload.

Hyperlapses.

Aggressive zooms.

Rapid cuts.

---

# Resolution

Prioritize the highest practical quality available.

Every asset should remain crisp on large desktop displays.

Never upscale visibly poor material.

Quality always has priority over quantity.

---

# Asset Selection Process

Before accepting any asset verify:

Emotional quality.

Editorial quality.

Technical quality.

Consistency with previous assets.

Cultural authenticity.

Composition.

Lighting.

Color harmony.

Narrative value.

If one criterion fails, continue searching or regenerate the asset.

---

# Visual Consistency

Two consecutive assets should feel like they belong to the same photographer.

Even if they originate from different sources.

Consistency is more important than variety.

---

# Asset Reuse

Avoid repeating the same visual subject excessively.

Avoid using nearly identical compositions.

Every new image should contribute something emotionally different.

Repetition should only occur intentionally.

---

# Naming Convention

Use descriptive, human-readable names.

Examples:

hero-earth-sunrise.avif

kyoto-street-evening.webp

traveler-writing-journal.avif

norway-fjord-panorama.avif

passport-editorial.webp

Avoid generic filenames.

Avoid random identifiers.

---

# Optimization

Every asset should be optimized before integration.

Compression must never noticeably reduce visual quality.

Visual fidelity always has priority over minimal file size.

---

# Final Rule

Before approving any visual asset ask one question.

> **Could this image appear in a premium travel magazine without anyone questioning its quality or authenticity?**

If the answer is yes, it belongs in Wander & Wonder.

If the answer is no, discard it and continue searching until a better asset is found.
